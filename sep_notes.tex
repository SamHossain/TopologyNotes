\textbf{Definition:} A topology on the set $\XX$ is defined as a collection of $U \subseteq \XX$ to form T such that: \newline (1) BOth the empty set and $\XX$ are in T. \newline (2) ANy union of elements of T is an element of T \newline (3) Any intersection of finitely many elements of T is an element of T
\textbf{Definition:} A set $A \subseteq \XX$, where X is endowed with the topology T is said to be open if $A \subset T$
\textbf{Definition:}Let X be a set. A basis for a topology on X is a collection $B$ of subsets on X such that: \newline (1) $B$ covers X \newline (2) If $x \in B_1 \cap B_2$ with $B_1, B_2 \in B$ then $\exists B_3 \in B$ such that $x \in B_3 \subset B_1 \cap B_2$
\begin{theorm}
        On a set X, lset $B$ and $B'$ be bases that generate T and $T'$respectively then $T'$ is finer than T $\Leftrightarrow \forall x \in X, B \in B$ containing x, $\exists B' \in \mathpzc{B'}$ such that $x \in B' \subset B$
\end{theorm}
\begin{proof}
        \Leftarrow Take $U \in T$ now since T is generated by B for all $x \in U \exists B \in \mathpzc{B}$ with $x \in B \subset U$. By hypothesis, $\exists B' \in \mathpzc{B}$ with $x \in B' \subset B$ so $x \in B' \subset U$. \newline 
        \Rightarrow Let $B \in \mathpzc{B}$ we have $B in T$. Since $T'$ is finer than T, we bbave $B \in T'$. Now since $B'$ generates the finer topology, we have $B' \in B'$ with $x \in B' \subset \mathpzc{B}.
